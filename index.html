The problem01 / 06

The operational layer is no longer the value layer.

Media agencies built their margin around a service that is now being automated. For two decades, the complexity of buying and placing advertising across channels justified significant fees and opacity. That complexity is no longer a barrier — AI platforms handle it natively and at cost.

The disruption is not coming. It has already moved into agency economics. What it exposes is a gap: the operational layer generated revenue without necessarily generating brand value. Plumb was built to fill that gap — to score what the machines can't price, and make the capital decision defensible before the IO is signed.

The conflict02 / 06

Three compensation models. One structural problem.

The holdcos' accountability gap is not a payment-model question. Every compensation structure produces a version of the same conflict: the agency's economic incentive is not fully aligned with the brand's media outcome. Plumb is structurally upstream of all three.

Model A

Percentage of spend

The agency earns a fee as a percentage of total media spend. Higher volume equals higher compensation. Reach and frequency are rewarded regardless of whether the inventory earns the brand's presence in it.
Structural incentive to over-buy. The bigger plan pays more, whether or not it scores COMMIT.
Model B

Principal buying

The agency acquires inventory at wholesale and resells to the client at a margin. They hold the position before the brand does. They are simultaneously advisor and counterparty — recommending inventory they already own.
Pushes inventory they hold, not inventory that scores best. The conflict is structural, not behavioral.
Model C

Performance-based

Compensation is tied to measurable outcomes: ROAS, CPA, click attribution. Sounds aligned. In practice it optimizes for what is attributable, not for what builds brand standing or earns cultural trust over time.
Incentivizes optimization of trackable signals. Authenticity and cultural truth are not in the attribution model.
The answer03 / 06

Three layers. One verdict. Receipts at flight close.

Layer 1

Optimization

CPM efficiency. Reach quality. Frequency fit. Targeting precision. Attribution clarity. The distribution floor. AI platforms automate this layer natively and at cost. It is necessary to score, but it is no longer sufficient to differentiate. Sourced from third-party audience data, platform rate cards, and measurement partner integrations.
Layer 2

Authenticity

Context fit. Audience dignity. Cultural truth. Earned placement. Moment respect. The layer that cannot be automated — because it is not in the bid stream and cannot be reduced to a parameter. Scored via structured rubric and senior buyer judgment on the soft cases.
Layer 3

Pricing

Budget fit. Cost per KPI. Scale threshold. Payback window. Reversibility cost. The gate. The CFO asks it — not whether the buy is efficient, but whether the capital decision is sound. A COMMIT at WRONG price renegotiates before IO.
The kernel04 / 06

Five states. One of them names the holdco trap.

Commit
All three layers earn the buy. Distribution math clears the floor, authenticity earns the moment, pricing is sound. Approve with a named one-way door criterion and a documented KPI target set.
Consult
One layer requires judgment the scoring model cannot supply — typically cultural truth or earned placement. Escalate to a named senior strategist. Override reasoning is logged as a permanent audit field. Documented escalations hit at 78%; undocumented at 33%.
Hold
One layer is soft but not disqualifying. A brief gap, incomplete data, or missing KPI set. Tighten the inputs and re-score before approving spend.
Wait
Pricing fails or both quality layers are below threshold. Not a media question yet. Return to strategy or renegotiate the rate before re-entering the scoring flow.
Toxic
High optimization, low authenticity. The buy scores well on the metrics the agency charges against — and poorly on the layer they have no incentive to measure. This is the structural conflict made visible. This is the verdict no agency compensation model will ever produce on its own.
The proof05 / 06

The lift is documented at flight close.

Every audit carries a number. Every number resolves at flight close against the KPI targets named at the time of the verdict. The start06 / 06

Run one audit against your next IO.

Plumb runs as a fixed-fee audit per buy. The first audit is on us. If the verdict does not change a buying decision, the engagement closes. If it does, the engagement scales to audit-as-a-service across the brand's full media plan — search, social, out-of-home, CTV, linear TV, radio, print, and the cross-channel coherence calls in between.

The framework is channel-agnostic. The conflict of interest is not. That asymmetry is the product.
